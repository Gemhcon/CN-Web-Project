Katsu Cod Recipe

Why not give your fish and chips a Japanese twist with cod coated in panko breadcrumbs and served with a Katsu Curry Sauce.

Ingredients

For The Cod

  • Frozen At Sea Atlantic Cod Loins 2 pack
  • 75g Plain Flour (seasoned with salt and pepper)
  • 1 eggbeaten
  • 100g Panko Breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable Oil for deep frying

For The Katsu Curry Sauce

  • 1 Onion finely sliced
  • 1 carrot finely sliced
  • 2 tbs vegetable oil
  • A thumb size piece of ginger grated
  • 2 tbs mild curry power
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tbs plain flour
  • 250ml chicken stock
  • 150ml coconut milk
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp light soy sauce

Method

For The Cod

  1. Put the eggs, flour and panko breadcrumbs in different bowls.
  2. Cut the cod loins in two so they can fit in the pan.
  3. Dip the cod loins into the flour first, then the egg, then into the Panko breadcrumbs - making sure they are well coated on each stage.
  4. Place coated cod on plate and losely cover in cling flim and place in the fridge foe at least 30 minutes .
  5. Whilst your cod is in the fridge, start the curry sauce (see below).
  6. When ready, half fill your pan with oil and heat to 190°C or until a piece of bread browns in 30 seconds. If it browns quicker it is too hot and you need to reduce the temp and try again.
  7. Once the oil is hot carefully add in two of the cod loins (don’t add all 4 as this will reduced the temperature of the oil).
  8. Fry for 4 to 6 minutes until the breadcrumbs are golden brown. Carefully remove and drain on kitchen roll or a cooling rack.

For The Katsu Curry Sauce

  1. Heat the oil in a pan.
  2. Add the finely sliced onions and carrots and fry until soft (around 5 minutes).
  3. Once soft add the grated ginger and crushed garlic and gently fry for a couple of minutes.
  4. Add the spices and gently fry for a minute.
  5. Stir in the flour and slowly add the stock stiring as you pour.
  6. Bring to the boil then reduce the heat and add in the coconut milk.
  7. Simmer for 5 minutes and add in the soy sauce and sugar.
  8. Stir in the flour and slowly add the stock stiring as you pour.
  9. If you like a smooth sauce, blend either with a hand blender or food processor.
  10. Keep warm until your cod is ready and serve together.
